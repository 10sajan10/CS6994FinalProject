Parquet dataset guide
=====================

Generator notebook
------------------

The current parquet files are generated by:

build_streamflow_training_parquet_v2_multihorizon.ipynb

The notebook connects to the local PostgreSQL database created by Docker,
extracts normalized observations, bins them to hourly series, creates supervised
multihorizon examples, and writes one parquet file per target site.

Shared row design
-----------------

Each row is one forecast initialization time for one site.

Shared metadata columns:

- site_id: target site identifier.
- history_end_time: final timestamp in the 168-hour lookback window.
- forecast_start_time: first timestamp being predicted.
- forecast_end_time: last timestamp in the 24-hour forecast horizon.
- year, month, day, hour, day_of_week, day_of_year: forecast-start calendar features.
- season: encoded forecast-start season.
  - 0 = winter
  - 1 = spring
  - 2 = summer
  - 3 = fall
- sample_stride_hours: sampling stride used when the row was created.

Sampling:

- Forecast horizon: 24 hourly targets.
- Lookback window: 168 hourly history values.
- Default stride: 6 hours.
- High-density months: March, April, May, June.
- High-density stride: 1 hour.


Discharge parquet schema
------------------------

Target sites:

- Site 3: Logan River at the Utah Water Research Laboratory west bridge.
- Site 4: Logan River at Mendon Road.

Discharge rows include:

- discharge_t-168 through discharge_t-1
  - Hourly target-site discharge history, oldest to newest.
- air_temp_avg_last_24h
  - Mean air temperature over the 24 hours before forecast start.
- precip_avg_last_24h
  - Mean precipitation over the 24 hours before forecast start.
- snow_depth_latest
  - Latest snow-depth value at the end of the history window.
- target_discharge_t+1 through target_discharge_t+24
  - Observed future discharge targets.

Support sites:

- Air temperature: climate site 2.
- Precipitation: climate site 2.
- Snow depth: climate site 1.
- Discharge history: the target stream site itself.

Current discharge parquet shapes from the notebook output:

- Site 3: 33,108 rows, 207 columns.
- Site 4: 32,730 rows, 207 columns.


Air-temperature parquet schema
------------------------------

Target sites:

- Site 1.
- Site 2.

Air-temperature rows include only:

- air_temp_t-168 through air_temp_t-1
  - Hourly target-site air-temperature history, oldest to newest.
- target_air_temp_t+1 through target_air_temp_t+24
  - Observed future air-temperature targets.
- Calendar metadata columns listed above.

No discharge, precipitation, snow depth, or external climate variables are used
for the air-temperature model inputs.


Data-quality rules
------------------

Discharge:

- Negative discharge values are treated as missing.
- Negative snow-depth values are treated as missing.
- Negative air-temperature values are allowed.
- Missing discharge, air temperature, and precipitation inputs are filled with
  time interpolation plus edge fill.
- Missing snow depth is filled by carry-forward and backfill.
- Future discharge target values must be observed, not imputed.
- A sample is dropped if discharge, air temperature, precipitation, or snow depth
  has a raw missing gap of 10 consecutive hours or more within the full
  history-plus-forecast window.

Air temperature:

- Negative air-temperature values are allowed.
- Missing air-temperature history inputs are filled with time interpolation plus
  edge fill.
- Future air-temperature target values must be observed, not imputed.
- A sample is dropped if air temperature has a raw missing gap of 10 consecutive
  hours or more within the full history-plus-forecast window.


Trainer-side loading rules
--------------------------

All trainers parse the timestamp columns and deduplicate rows by:

- site_id
- forecast_start_time

When duplicates exist, the trainer keeps the newest source based on available
version columns and source_mtime. The source_path and source_mtime columns are
loader metadata and are not used as model features.
